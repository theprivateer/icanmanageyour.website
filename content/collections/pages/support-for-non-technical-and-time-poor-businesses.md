    content: |-
      I typically work with:

      ### Small business owners with limited time

      You might be wearing five different hats already. Marketing, operations, sales, admin. The website often becomes "something I'll deal with later" until something goes wrong.

      I make sure it doesn't get to that point.

      ### Teams without in-house technical staff

      Many growing businesses have great people in marketing, admin, or operations, but no dedicated web or technical resource. When something breaks, it becomes a scramble.

      Instead of bouncing between freelancers or generic support desks, you have one consistent point of contact.

      ### Businesses that rely on their website but don't want to manage it

      If your website drives leads, processes bookings, or supports customers, it's too important to ignore. But that doesn't mean you want to be hands-on with it.

      You can focus on running your business. I'll focus on keeping your site healthy.

      ### Organisations that need a dependable partner

      Sometimes what you really need is someone who understands your website and your business context. Someone who can give clear advice, not just technical answers.

      That's the difference between "support" and a working relationship.
